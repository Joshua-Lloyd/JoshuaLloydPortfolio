What are Robots?

Robots are programmable machines designed to do a job. It does this by gathering information from its surroundings (sensing) then using the information to perform the appropriate actions to do its job.

All robots have a purpose. Different purposes exist for different robots: some robots are built to clean; others are built to perform repetitive assembly work; others are built to pick up heavy objects; other are even built for entertainment.

Because of this disparity, robots can come in many different shapes and sizes, but there are underlying principles that all robots have in common.

The Different Types of Robots

Industrial Robots

Industrial robots are ones used in a manufacturing environment for processes such as automated sorting, assembly, machining and fabrication, or painting. There are many kinds of industrial robots but the most recognized form is the robotic arm. Companies use industrial robots on their production/assembly lines because they're a cheaper and more efficient form of labour compared to humans. They also make less mistakes because they're programmed to perform the same action continuously over long periods.

Domestic Robots

Domestic robots are ones that tend to be used by consumers instead of businesses. They can have a multitude of uses, including window cleaning, vacuum cleaning, sweeping, and even telepresence and surveillance.

Military Robots

Robots used for military applications. There exist robots that diffuse bombs and soon there will be robots that are capable of carrying cargo. Some countries even own sentries that fire at moving targets.

Other kinds

There are also robots used in a research environment and robots designed to help surgeons perform intricate tasks through a user-interface and end-effector. There are also robots built for entertainment like RC drones, robot dogs and dinos, and line-followers.
